About Me

I'm Patrick, a 20-year-old Computer Science student at TU Graz entering my third semester. I am genuinely driven by this field and love bridging the gap between university theory and actual implementation.

For me, it all comes down to problem-solving. I've always been drawn to difficult, focused challenges, whether it's a challenging algorithm, a data structure bottleneck, or a weird bug. Before deciding on CS, I actually considered mechanical or electrical engineering because I wanted to build things. I ultimately chose software because I think it's the pinnacle of problem-solving, you get to build systems without being constrained by physical limitations.

My academic work gives me a strong foundation in C and C++, which taught me how software manages memory under the hood. Beyond university, I spent three months at AVL List GmbH, working on core software products using Python, and getting hands-on experience with Agile workflows, Scrum, and Jira. That industry experience actually motivated me to go to university. I wanted to step back and get a deeper, fundamental understanding of Computer Science and Software Engineering.

You can find more about my tech-stack on my GitHub.
